🧠 About Minds

Nowadays, there are two kinds of minds: biological and machine.

  • Biological minds — like the brains of humans and animals.
  • Machine minds — like modern AI systems such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Grok.

The main job of every mind — whether biological or machine — is to learn and think.

And here’s something surprising: at their core, learning and thinking are actually processes of doing MATH. Yes, really! And it’s SIMPLE MATH!

We don’t just build AI — since it learns on its own, it’s more like we are GROWING each AI MODEL.

How is this possible? In this course, you’ll discover the answer.

The Building Blocks Of Every Mind

Every mind is, in essence, a NEURAL NETWORK.

It’s called "NEURAL" because it’s made up of NEURONS.

😎
NEURON

And it’s called "NETWORK" because the NEURONS connect to each other.

We can think of each NEURON as a tiny yet cool creation.

But what does every NEURON actually do?

Every NEURON takes in input SIGNALS and then sends out one output SIGNAL:

input SIGNAL input SIGNAL

output SIGNAL

What is a SIGNAL?

Generally, a SIGNAL is just anything — like an electric impulse — that can be measured or calculated and then represented as a number. For example: 0.53 or 0.61.

At this point, we don’t need to worry about exactly how SIGNALS are measured or calculated. We just need to remember that:

Every SIGNAL is something that can be represented as a number.

In more detail, every NEURON:

  • Takes in input SIGNALS (say, 0.53 and 0.61)
  • Does simple math on them to calculate its output SIGNAL (say, 0.12)
  • Then sends out this output SIGNAL

For brevity:

  • The input SIGNALS are called INPUTS.
  • The output SIGNAL is called OUTPUT.

The Key Insight

An OUTPUT from one NEURON can become an INPUT for another NEURON that comes next — connecting the whole team together!

Scale: Billions of NEURONS

There may be up to billions of NEURONS in a single neural network — whether in a human brain or in a large AI model such as a Transformer.

The Math Every Neuron Does

When a mind thinks, every NEURON really does very simple math.

At its core, every NEURON works just like a shopping bill.

Analogy: The Shopping Bill

Imagine buying donuts and cupcakes:

shopping bill
UNITS PRICE PER UNIT AMOUNT
donut 4 2.00 8.00
cupcake 2 3.00 6.00
SUM 14.00
DISCOUNT -0.50
TOTAL 13.50

The math is simple:

TOTAL = 4.00 × 2.00 + 2.00 × 3.00 + (-0.50)
TOTAL = 8.00 + 6.00 - 0.50
TOTAL = 14.00 - 0.50
TOTAL = 13.50

This math can also be represented this way:

4.002.00UNITS
2.003.00PRICES
8.006.00AMOUNTS
14.00SUM
-0.50DISCOUNT
13.50TOTAL

At its core, the shopping bill is just a formula:

TOTAL = UNITS1 × PRICE1 + UNITS2 × PRICE2 + DISCOUNT
TOTAL = 4.00 × 2.00 + 2.00 × 3.00 + (-0.50)
TOTAL = 8.00 + 6.00 - 0.50
TOTAL = 14.00 - 0.50
TOTAL = 13.50

In terms of a NEURON, the same math can be represented this way:

4.002.00INPUTS
2.003.00WEIGHTS
8.006.00PRODUCTS
14.00SUM
-0.50BIAS
13.50OUTPUT

At its core, every NEURON is also just a formula:

OUTPUT = INPUT1 × WEIGHT1 + INPUT2 × WEIGHT2 + BIAS
OUTPUT = 4.00 × 2.00 + 2.00 × 3.00 + (-0.50)
OUTPUT = 8.00 + 6.00 - 0.50
OUTPUT = 14.00 - 0.50
OUTPUT = 13.50

Ultra simple. No math changed. Only names changed.

TOTAL = 21.00 - 0.50
TOTAL = 20.50

The mapping:

Shopping Bill → NEURON
UNITS→INPUTS
PRICES→WEIGHTS
AMOUNTS→PRODUCTS
SUM→SUM
DISCOUNT→BIAS
TOTAL→OUTPUT

The WEIGHTS and BIAS are called PARAMETERS.

If you understand this, you already understand a lot of AI.
